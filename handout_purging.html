Purging During Medicine Sessions
What it is, why it happens, and how to work with it as one of the most powerful healing moments of your journey.

Purging during a medicine session is not a side effect to be managed, it is a release to be honored. Many traditions consider it one of the deepest forms of healing available.

What Is Purging?
🌊
More Than Physical
Understanding the Experience
In the context of medicine sessions, purging refers to any form of physical or emotional release, most commonly nausea or vomiting, but also crying, shaking, yawning, coughing, laughing uncontrollably, sweating, or even passing gas. All of these are the body's way of moving something out that no longer belongs.

Purging is common with psilocybin, ayahuasca, and other plant medicines. It can also occur with ketamine, particularly in the first session or at higher doses, especially in people who carry significant stored tension, trauma, or suppressed emotion in the body.
If you feel nausea coming on during your session, do not fight it. Fighting increases discomfort. Allowing moves you through it faster.
🏺
Ancient Wisdom
Shamanic Tradition
Across indigenous healing traditions, from Amazonian ayahuasca ceremonies to Andean plant medicine circles, purging has always been considered sacred and deeply intentional. It is called "la purga" in many traditions, meaning simply: the purge. It is not seen as an embarrassing side effect. It is seen as the medicine doing its deepest work.

Healers and curanderos often say that what leaves the body during a purge is not just food, it is old grief, ancestral pain, stagnant energy, fear, and trauma that the body has been holding long before the medicine arrived.
In many ceremonies, purging is met with gratitude and even celebration by the group. It is a sign that the medicine has found what it came for.
Why It Happens
🧠
The Body Stores What the Mind Cannot Process
Somatic Science
The body is not just a vehicle for the mind, it is a storage system for unprocessed experience. When trauma, grief, fear, or chronic stress overwhelms our capacity to process it consciously, the nervous system buries it in the body, in the gut, the diaphragm, the throat, the chest.

Manifesting medicines create a state of heightened neuroplasticity and somatic openness in which the body finally has the safety and the signal to release what it has been holding. Purging is often the body choosing the most direct route, out through the gut, which carries more neural tissue than almost any other organ in the body.
The gut contains over 100 million nerve cells, more than the spinal cord. It is sometimes called the "second brain." Releasing through the gut is releasing through a major emotional processing center.
🔥
Physical Triggers
Also Normal
Beyond the emotional and energetic, there are also straightforward physical reasons purging can occur: the medicine itself may be activating the vagus nerve or the gut's serotonin receptors · eating too close to the session · the body's response to altered states and changes in blood pressure · deep breathing patterns during the session activating the diaphragm and releasing held tension. None of these make the experience less meaningful. The physical and the emotional are not separate.
Working With It Intentionally
🎯
Use Intention as Your Anchor
The Most Important Shift
The single most powerful thing you can do when purging begins is to give it a name and a direction. Instead of reacting with panic or shame, meet the sensation with a conscious intention:

"I am releasing the grief I have been carrying since losing my patient."
"I am purging the helplessness I felt during those years of burnout."
"I am letting go of what no longer belongs in my body."

You do not need to know exactly what you are releasing. You can simply say: "Whatever this is, I am ready to let it go." This shifts the experience from something happening to you into something you are choosing, and that shift changes everything.
Your facilitator will have a bucket or bowl nearby. There is no need to hold back or feel embarrassed. This space holds all of it.
🌬️
Breathe Into It, Not Away From It
In the Moment
When nausea or the urge to purge rises, the instinct is to tighten, to clench the belly, hold the breath, fight it back. This almost always makes it worse and prolongs the discomfort.

Instead, open to it with your breath. Breathe slowly into the belly. Relax the jaw. Drop the shoulders. Let the wave come. Waves always crest and then pass. The faster you stop resisting, the faster it moves through you, and often, what follows the purge is a profound and immediate sense of lightness, clarity, and relief.
🔊
Let Sound Come With It
Vocal Release
Some of the most powerful purges are not silent. Moaning, crying, wailing, or making sound as you release activates the vagus nerve, opens the throat, and allows stored emotion to move through the body more completely. You do not need to perform anything, simply allow whatever sound arises naturally to come with the release.

In many healing traditions, sound and purging together are considered the fullest form of emotional release available in a medicine space.
You will not disturb anyone. Your facilitator has held this space many times. Let yourself be fully human here.
✨
After the Purge
What Often Follows
People who work with their purge intentionally often describe what follows as some of the most profound moments of their entire session, sudden clarity, deep waves of peace, a feeling of having put down something extraordinarily heavy, connection to parts of themselves they had not felt in years.

Rest in it fully. Do not rush back into the journey. Place a hand on your heart or your belly. Breathe gently. Acknowledge what just happened. You released something real. That deserves a moment of quiet honor.
Preparing Before Your Session
🍽️
Food, Water & Timing
Practical Preparation
Fasting before your session reduces the likelihood of nausea. For ketamine sessions, follow your provider's specific instructions, typically nothing by mouth for a set window before your session. For plant medicine ceremonies, a light diet or full fast in the days before is traditional and evidence-supported.

Stay well hydrated in the days leading up to your session but avoid drinking large amounts of water immediately before. Ginger tea before a session can reduce nausea without suppressing the experience.
💭
Set an Intention for Your Release
Intention Work
In the days before your session, consider sitting quietly and asking yourself: "What am I ready to release?"

You might identify a specific emotion, a memory, a pattern, a relationship dynamic, or simply "the weight I have been carrying." Write it down. Bring it with you into the session. If purging comes, you will already have language for what you are letting go, and that language makes the release exponentially more powerful.
You cannot force a purge. But you can prepare your body and mind to welcome one if it comes, and that welcome changes the experience entirely.
✦ A Word to Helpers
Many of you have spent years holding other people's pain without ever releasing your own. You have suppressed your own grief in exam rooms, swallowed your own fear in trauma bays, and pushed through your own exhaustion with professionalism and grace.

The purge is not weakness. It is the body finally being allowed to do what it has needed to do for a very long time.

If it comes for you, welcome it. You have earned this release. You do not have to hold it anymore.
