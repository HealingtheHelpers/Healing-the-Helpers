Books · Psychedelics & Consciousness
1.The Ketamine Breakthrough Dr. Mike Dow and Ronan Levy · the most accessible guide to ketamine-assisted psychotherapy for both clients and therapists, written as the go-to manual for KAP, includes a session-by-session workbook · described as the most important breakthrough in mental health since Prozac
2.How to Change Your Mind Michael Pollan · the book that brought psychedelic science to mainstream conversation, blending personal experience with rigorous research
3.The Doors of Perception Aldous Huxley · the 1954 classic account of Huxley's mescaline experience that changed how the Western world thought about consciousness
4.Healing Psychedelics Micah Stover · innovative therapies for trauma and transformation, written for practitioners and the people who work with them
5.Psychedelic Therapy Will Van Derveer, MD and Keith Kurlander, MA, LPC · a comprehensive clinical guide to restoring mental health and reclaiming your life through psychedelic-assisted approaches
6.This Is Your Mind on Plants Michael Pollan · shorter and more accessible than How to Change Your Mind, focused on opium, caffeine, and mescaline
7.The Psychedelic Experience Timothy Leary, Ralph Metzner, and Richard Alpert · the original 1964 guide based on the Tibetan Book of the Dead · foundational and still essential
8.DMT: The Spirit Molecule Rick Strassman, MD · the research behind endogenous DMT and what it reveals about consciousness
9.When the Impossible Happens Stanislav Grof, MD · his most readable book, written as personal stories rather than academic theory, covering fifty years of extraordinary research into LSD, Holotropic Breathwork, consciousness after death, and the full landscape of non-ordinary reality · the best entry point into his work
10.How Psychedelics Work: The Hidden Mind and Our Power to Heal Robin Carhart-Harris · brand new 2025 · the world's leading psychedelic neuroscientist's first book, covering the brain science, the research, and the future of the field
Books · Trauma & Healing
1.Healing the Helper: The Hidden Costs of Helping LaTisha Carter · written directly for clinicians, healthcare workers, educators, caregivers, and anyone who carries everyone else · gives language, validation, and practical tools to the helpers who quietly burn out while showing up for the world
2.The Myth of Normal Gabor Maté, MD · Maté's most comprehensive work, exploring how trauma and disconnection are at the root of most illness and what genuine healing actually requires
3.In the Realm of Hungry Ghosts Gabor Maté, MD · his most famous book, on addiction and the unmet childhood needs that drive compulsive behavior, written from years working with Vancouver's street-addicted population · one of the most compassionate books ever written on why people use and what healing actually requires
4.When the Body Says No Gabor Maté, MD · explores the connection between emotional repression and serious illness including cancer and autoimmune disease · predates The Myth of Normal and is more focused and equally powerful
5.No Bad Parts Richard C. Schwartz, PhD · the foundational IFS book, accessible to everyone, reframes the inner critic and every difficult part of you as a protector with a story worth hearing
6.The Body Keeps the Score Bessel van der Kolk, MD · the defining book on trauma and the body, explaining why talk therapy alone is often not enough and what actually helps the nervous system heal
7.The Biology of Trauma Amy Apigian, MD · a clinical and deeply practical look at how fear lives in the body and the specific protocols that help it complete and release
8.Somatic Internal Family Systems Therapy Susan McConnell · weaves breath, movement, touch, and awareness into IFS work, essential reading for body-based practitioners
9.The Autoimmune Cure Sara Gottfried, MD · makes the case that unresolved trauma is the missing piece in most autoimmune illness, with a practical healing framework
10.Waking the Tiger Peter A. Levine, PhD · the book that introduced Somatic Experiencing to the world, translated into 22 languages, explaining how the body holds trauma and how physical sensation and movement can release it when talk therapy cannot · the foundational somatic trauma text
11.Into the Magic Shop James Doty, MD · a neurosurgeon's memoir of the summer a stranger taught him to train his mind and open his heart, and how it changed the trajectory of his entire life
