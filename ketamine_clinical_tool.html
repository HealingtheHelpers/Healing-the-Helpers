⚠️ Important: Do Not Swallow

When using sublingual troches or rapid dissolve tablets, swallowing the accumulated saliva is not acceptable. Many clients are simply never told this. Swallowing produces 16–30x higher urinary metabolite concentrations and significantly increases bladder and bile duct exposure. Every client must be explicitly instructed: spit the saliva throughout — do not swallow it.

CONSIDER GOING UP
Heavy regular alcohol use
Chronic alcohol upregulates NMDA receptors. Clients who drink heavily may metabolize ketamine faster and respond less at standard doses.
Subtherapeutic response at prior dose
If previous sessions were well tolerated but effect was minimal, a modest upward adjustment beyond the calculator may be warranted.
Previous session shorter than expected
If the session lasted significantly less than typical — under 30 min for IM/SQ or under 45 min for sublingual/IN — this may indicate rapid CYP2B6 metabolism. Consider a modest upward adjustment and document the response.
CONSIDER CANDIDACY
Liver impairment
Elevated LFTs, hepatitis, or cirrhosis will slow ketamine metabolism. Consider whether this client is an appropriate candidate at all before proceeding.
Cardiovascular history
Uncontrolled hypertension, arrhythmia, or significant CAD may make ketamine inappropriate entirely. Consider candidacy before scheduling.
CNS depressants on board
If gabapentin, sleep meds, antipsychotics, or benzos were not held as recommended, consider postponing the session rather than proceeding.
⚠️ Clinical Reminder

This tool provides a starting point based on weight-based dosing protocol. Always apply clinical judgment. Check blood pressure before dosing and at 20–30 minutes. Consult your supervising physician for any client outside standard parameters.
