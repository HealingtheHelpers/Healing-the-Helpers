Understanding Your Nervous System
Your autonomic nervous system runs beneath conscious awareness, regulating every stress response, moment of connection, and period of rest in your body. Understanding it is the first step to healing it.

The Foundation of Trauma-Informed Healing , your nervous system is not broken. It is doing exactly what it learned to do. And it can learn something new.

The Autonomic Nervous System at a Glance
Sympathetic Parasympathetic
Healthy Expression Play, excitement, movement, engagement, mobilizing for joy True rest, deep sleep, digestion, restoration, recovery
Threat Expression Fight or flight, hypervigilance, anxiety, overwhelm Freeze, collapse, shutdown, dissociation, faint response
Body Experience Heart racing, muscles activated, adrenaline, alertness Slowing down, digestion, calm, warmth, heaviness
Common Misconception "Sympathetic is always bad" "Parasympathetic is always good"
The Truth You need sympathetic activation to play, move, and feel alive Collapse and freeze are also parasympathetic. Rest and digest must be cultivated.
✦ The Goal Is Not More Parasympathetic
The goal is healthy expressions of both, and the capacity to move fluidly between them.

Excitement and anxiety feel similar in the body. Learning to tell the difference between mobilizing for joy versus mobilizing for threat is one of the most powerful skills in nervous system healing.
When the System Gets Stuck , Dysautonomia
⚡
What Is Dysautonomia?
Chronic Dysregulation
Chronic stress and trauma can dysregulate the autonomic nervous system , a condition called dysautonomia. The system loses its ability to shift fluidly between activation and rest, becoming stuck in patterns of hyperarousal, hypoarousal, or rapid cycling between both.
Common Symptoms of Dysautonomia
Fatigue and low energyBrain fog and difficulty concentrating
Sleep disturbancesLow tolerance for exercise
Lightheadedness or dizziness (POTS)Increased vulnerability to anxiety
Reduced capacity to recover from stressEmotional dysregulation
What Helps , Building Nervous System Capacity
🌊
Somatic & Body-Based Practices
Foundation
Movement, breathwork, shaking, and touch work directly with the nervous system below the level of conscious thought , completing stress cycles, releasing held activation, and restoring the body's capacity to shift between states. The body is not a vehicle for the mind. It is where healing actually happens.
📈
Titrated Stress Exposure
Building Resilience
The nervous system builds capacity the same way a muscle does , through graduated exposure to stress followed by adequate recovery time. Too much stress without recovery leads to breakdown. Titrated exposure with rest leads to a wider window of tolerance and greater resilience over time.
🤝
Co-regulation
Relational Healing
Safe relationships are one of the most powerful nervous system regulators available. The nervous system is a social organ , it evolved to be regulated in connection with others. Co-regulation means allowing another person's calm, settled nervous system to help your own find its way back to balance. This is why therapeutic presence, retreat community, and safe relationships are not luxuries , they are medicine.
✨
Ketamine-Assisted Healing
Reset & Rewire
Ketamine has been shown to reset dysregulated nervous system patterns through its action on the glutamate system and its induction of rapid neuroplasticity. By temporarily quieting the default mode network and creating a window of heightened neural reorganization, ketamine-assisted healing allows the nervous system to break out of entrenched patterns of dysregulation and form new, healthier pathways.
Ketamine is the only legally available medicine in the US currently shown to reset autonomic dysregulation at the neural level.
🌿
Nature, Play & Creativity
Natural Regulators
Time in nature, unstructured play, and creative expression are ancient nervous system regulators , wired into the human system long before clinical interventions existed. Research on forest bathing, awe, and play all show measurable reductions in cortisol and sympathetic activation. These are not indulgences. They are evidence-based nervous system medicine.

The Triune Brain Model
✦ Neurological Building Blocks for Body-Mind Health
The brain operates in three functional layers. Each layer has a primary question it is always asking. Healing work engages all three, bottom up and top down. Note: this is a simplified model, but clinically useful for understanding why trauma disrupts cognition, emotion, and survival responses in sequence.
PFC
Frontal Cortex (PFC)
Creativity and Consciousness
Primary question: What can I learn?

The prefrontal cortex governs executive function, creativity, consciousness, rational thought, empathy, and decision-making. It is the seat of your clinical judgment and relational attunement.

When the lower brain layers are in threat mode, the PFC goes offline. This is why trauma and burnout impair judgment, creativity, and compassion, not because helpers stop caring, but because the threat-detecting brain has hijacked the thinking brain.
Safety restores PFC function. Regulation is not soft, it is the prerequisite for competence.
LIM
Limbic System
Emotions and Memory
Primary question: Am I loved?

The limbic system processes emotion, relational safety, attachment, and emotional memory. It holds the record of every relationship, safe or unsafe, and uses that record to evaluate every new interaction.

Trauma lives here. So does connection, love, and the felt sense of belonging. Helpers with relational trauma histories often have a limbic system running old threat assessments in current safe relationships.
Ketamine-assisted healing works in part by temporarily quieting limbic threat reactivity, creating a window for new relational and emotional learning.
BS
Brain Stem
Reflexes and Survival
Primary question: Am I safe?

The brain stem controls survival functions, heart rate, breathing, reflexes, arousal. It operates below conscious awareness and responds to threat before you have a chance to think about it.

When the brain stem detects danger, it overrides everything above it. All the PFC training in the world cannot override a brain stem that believes it is under threat. This is why self-regulation must be practiced in the body, not just understood in the mind.
Bottom-up healing (somatic, breathwork, movement) works directly with the brain stem. Top-down healing (talk therapy, insight) works with the PFC. Both are needed. Neither alone is enough.
✦ A Note for Helpers
Your nervous system learned to be a first responder, always on, always ready, always scanning. That adaptation saved lives. But it was never meant to run forever without rest. This work teaches your body what safety feels like again, and slowly rebuilds your capacity to live there.
